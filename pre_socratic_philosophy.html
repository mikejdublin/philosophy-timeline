Pre-Socratic Philosophy from 624 to 450 BCE

Pre-Socratic philosophy refers to the philosophical thought that occurred before the time of Socrates in ancient Greece. These philosophers were concerned with explaining the nature of the world and the place of humans within it. They sought to understand the fundamental principles that govern the universe and the relationship between the natural world and the divine. Pre-Socratic philosophers made significant contributions to the development of logic and argumentation, and were the first to attempt to explain the world in terms of natural rather than supernatural causes. They continue to be studied and debated by philosophers today.
