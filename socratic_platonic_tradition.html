The Socratic and Platonic Tradition from 624 to 450 BCE

The Socratic and Platonic Tradition refers to the philosophical ideas and teachings of Socrates and his most famous student, Plato. The focus of this tradition is on ethics, with the belief that the ultimate goal of human existence is the attainment of moral perfection. Central to this tradition is the concept of the Forms or Ideas, which are the ultimate reality behind the material world. Plato argued that knowledge of the Forms was necessary for true understanding and that this knowledge could only be obtained through a process of philosophical inquiry.
