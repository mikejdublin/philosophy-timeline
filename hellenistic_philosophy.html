Hellenistic Philosophy from 323 to 31 BCE

Hellenistic philosophy refers to the philosophical thought that occurred during the Hellenistic period in ancient Greece and the Near East. This period was marked by a focus on practical concerns and the application of philosophical ideas to everyday life. Hellenistic philosophers, such as Epicurus and the Stoics, developed systems of ethics that focused on living a good life and achieving happiness. The Hellenistic period also saw the development of skepticism, which is the idea that certain knowledge is not possible and that we should suspend judgment on certain questions. Hellenistic philosophers were also interested in the nature of the self and the role of the individual in society. Hellenistic philosophy continues to be a subject of discussion and debate among philosophers today.
